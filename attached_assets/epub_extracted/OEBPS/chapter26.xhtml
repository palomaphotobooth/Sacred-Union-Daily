CHAPTER 26

Living Daily From Union With God

Throughout this journey we have explored an idea that lies at the heart of Christian mysticism and theology: the invitation to live in union with God. For many believers, the spiritual life has often been understood primarily as a series of efforts to reach God—through prayer, discipline, worship, or moral improvement. While these practices are valuable, they can sometimes create the impression that God remains distant and must be pursued through constant striving. Yet the message of the Gospel reveals something quite different. Through Christ, reconciliation between God and humanity has already been accomplished. The barrier that once seemed to separate humanity from God has been removed. The Holy Spirit now dwells within believers, making God's presence not distant but intimately near. Union with God, therefore, is not something believers must achieve. It is something they are invited to recognize. Living from union means beginning each day with the awareness that God's presence is already part of one's life. Instead of striving to bring God into every situation, believers learn to notice that God is already present within those moments. This shift in perspective can transform the way everyday life is experienced. Morning prayer becomes less about asking God to come near and more about acknowledging the presence that has been there all along. Work becomes an opportunity to express love and service rather than simply a task to complete. Conversations become moments where compassion and grace can flow naturally. Even quiet moments of rest become reminders that life itself is sustained by God's presence. Living from union also changes how believers approach challenges. Difficult circumstances often create feelings of isolation or anxiety. When problems appear overwhelming, people may feel as though they must face those struggles alone. Yet the awareness of union reminds believers that God's presence accompanies them even in the most difficult moments. Instead of responding only with fear, believers learn to pause and remember that they are not alone. This awareness often brings a sense of calm that allows clearer thinking and wiser decisions. Living from union also encourages a deeper sense of trust. If God's presence is already woven into every moment of life, then believers can release some of the pressure to control every outcome. They can move forward with confidence that God's guidance and grace will continue to unfold along the journey. This does not mean that life becomes free from uncertainty or difficulty. Rather, it means that uncertainty is no longer faced alone. Union also reshapes identity. Many people define themselves by their accomplishments, failures, or the expectations of others. These definitions can create a constant sense of pressure. Living from union invites believers to see themselves differently. Their identity is rooted in relationship with God. They are people loved by God, sustained by grace, and invited into a life of compassion and service. From this foundation, actions begin to flow naturally. Kindness toward others becomes an expression of the love already experienced. Forgiveness becomes possible because believers themselves have received forgiveness. Generosity becomes easier because life is no longer driven by fear of scarcity. Union also deepens joy. When believers recognize God's presence in everyday life, ordinary moments take on new significance. A quiet morning, a meaningful conversation, a moment of beauty in nature—all become reminders of the life that flows from God. Joy becomes less dependent on external circumstances and more rooted in the steady awareness of God's nearness. This way of living does not require extraordinary spiritual experiences. It grows gradually through daily awareness. Each moment becomes an opportunity to remember the truth that has been present all along: God is near. As believers continue to live from this awareness, the spiritual life becomes less about striving and more about participation. Participation in the love of God. Participation in the grace that sustains life. Participation in the ongoing story of redemption that continues to unfold throughout the world. Living from union is therefore not a destination that one reaches and completes. It is a way of living—a continual rediscovery of God's presence in every moment.

Reflection & Practice

Reflect on these words from Jesus: “Abide in me, and I in you.” — John 15:4 Consider the following questions: How might your daily life change if you began each day remembering that God's presence is already near? In what moments of your day do you most easily become aware of God's presence? What practices help you return to that awareness when life becomes busy? Take a moment to pause, breathe slowly, and simply remember: You are not alone. God's presence is already woven into the fabric of your life. Living from that awareness is the beginning of a deeper spiritual journey.
