CHAPTER 19

The Gift of Silence

In a world filled with constant sound and activity, silence can feel unfamiliar. Most people spend their days surrounded by conversation, music, news, notifications, and endless streams of information. Even moments of rest are often filled with background noise or digital distractions. Because of this, silence sometimes feels uncomfortable at first. Yet throughout Christian history, silence has been recognized as one of the most powerful environments for spiritual awareness. Silence creates space. It allows the mind to settle and the heart to become attentive in ways that are difficult when life is constantly filled with noise. Many of the great spiritual teachers throughout history discovered that silence was not empty. Instead, silence often became the place where the presence of God felt most noticeable. This insight appears in scripture as well. One of the most famous examples occurs in the story of the prophet Elijah. During a time of great exhaustion and discouragement, Elijah sought an encounter with God. The story describes several dramatic events—a powerful wind, an earthquake, and a fire. Yet the text tells us that God's voice was not found in those dramatic displays. Instead, God spoke through what is often translated as a gentle whisper or a still small voice. This moment reveals something important about how divine presence is often experienced. God is not always encountered through dramatic events or powerful displays. More often, the presence of God becomes clear in quiet attentiveness. Silence allows the distractions of life to settle so that the heart can become more aware. For this reason, many contemplative traditions encourage believers to intentionally create moments of silence within their daily lives. These moments do not need to be long. Even a few minutes of quiet reflection can open space for deeper awareness. In silence, people often begin to notice things they previously overlooked. Thoughts that were racing through the mind begin to slow. Emotions that were buried beneath busyness become visible. Sometimes peace appears unexpectedly. At other times, silence reveals areas of worry or tension that had been hidden beneath constant activity. Both experiences can be valuable. Silence allows believers to bring their whole inner life into the presence of God. Instead of constantly pushing thoughts away or filling every moment with distraction, silence creates room for honesty and reflection. Over time, silence begins to feel less uncomfortable. It becomes a place of rest. Many people discover that even brief moments of quiet during the day can bring clarity and calm. Silence also helps deepen other spiritual practices. Prayer becomes more attentive when it includes moments of listening. Scripture reading becomes more meaningful when the mind has space to reflect. Gratitude becomes more noticeable when the heart is not overwhelmed by constant stimulation. Silence creates an environment where spiritual awareness can grow naturally. This does not mean that every silent moment will produce a dramatic spiritual experience. Often silence simply feels peaceful. Yet over time, this quiet awareness helps believers recognize that God is present even when life is calm and ordinary. In fact, many spiritual teachers have observed that the most profound awareness of God often appears in simple moments of stillness rather than in dramatic emotional experiences. Silence also teaches patience. Modern culture often expects quick results and immediate answers. But spiritual growth tends to unfold slowly. Silence invites believers to embrace that slower rhythm. Instead of rushing toward conclusions or forcing spiritual experiences, believers learn to wait attentively. This waiting is not passive. It is a posture of trust. It reflects the belief that God is already present and active, even when nothing dramatic appears to be happening. Over time, silence becomes less like an empty space and more like a quiet meeting place. It becomes a moment where believers remember that

God is near.

Reflection & Practice

Find a quiet place where you can sit comfortably for a few minutes. Close your eyes and take several slow breaths. Allow yourself to become aware of the quiet around you. You do not need to say anything or think about anything in particular. Simply rest in the silence. If your mind begins to wander, gently return your attention to the present moment. Remember the words from the Psalms: “Be still and know that I am God.” — Psalm 46:10 Allow those words to settle quietly in your mind. Often, the awareness of God's presence becomes clearer when we give ourselves permission to be still.
